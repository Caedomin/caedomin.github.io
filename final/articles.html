Exercise Does More Good Than You Think

Everyone knows exercise is “good for the body” but how many of us know why?

According to an article “Exercise for Mental Health” written in the Official Journal of the Association of Medicine and Psychiatry, evidence suggests that small amounts of consistent physical activity can largely benefit both physical and mental health.1 In this article, 9 points were written on how physical health can be improved:

  1. Improved Sleep
  2. Increased interest in intimacy
  3. Better endurance
  4. Stress relief
  5. Improvement in mood
  6. Increased energy and stamina
  7. Reduced tiredness that can increase mental alertness
  8. Weight reduction
  9. Reduced cholesterol and improved cardiovascular fitness
